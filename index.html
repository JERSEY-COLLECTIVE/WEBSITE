I wince,

snow washes over my retina

lattice cells over the present moment.

everything is time, everything is everything

I wanted to tell you how the first snow seemed

to freeze time, but you were missing.


Greens and reds spin over the dunes,

there is blood on her clothes, maybe something

to grow from as well.


As you step forward, close in on the wound

that splits the earth in two. Time moves faster.

The wet breath of cars murmuring in the distant light.

I ran into the esophageal woods I called out for their voices,

but the response was Time, blaring horns shouting to me

with many directions.


I laid, until I too was metabolized into the green and red,

until I was one from two, a collection of breaths, with you.
